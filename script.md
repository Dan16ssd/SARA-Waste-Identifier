## THE PROBLEM — Slides 7–8 · NAT · ~75 sec
*(Targets: Project Innovation — Problem-Oriented Approach.)*

### Slide 7 — The Problem

**NAT:** Let us show you the scale of what we are facing.

**[Pause. Let the numbers land one by one.]**

Laos generates six thousand nine hundred tonnes of household waste every single day. Vientiane alone produces fifteen percent of it. The Lower Mekong countries produced roughly eight million tonnes of plastic waste in 2020 — and seventy to ninety percent of it was bottles, bags, and styrofoam. Vientiane's waste has nearly doubled in the past decade, and the World Bank projects national waste will reach 1.4 million tonnes a year by 2035.

And here is the part that matters most: almost none of this is measured. Waste in Laos is managed by open dumping, open burning, and river discharge — decided blindly, with no data at all.

### Slide 8 — Why Existing Solutions Fail

**NAT:** Why hasn't this been solved? Manual systems are slow, expensive, and produce no usable data. Generic recycling apps were never built for Laos — no local disposal context, no campus-level data, and they demand app-store downloads that low-end phones struggle with. And structurally: nobody, anywhere in Southeast Asia, has built a campus-scale, real-time waste digital twin.

That gap is exactly where S.A.R.A. enters.

## THE SOLUTION — Slides 9–14 · ABE + NAJ · ~2 min 15 sec
*(Targets: Project Innovation — Goal-Oriented Approach, Innovation Outcomes.)*

### Slide 9 — Our Solution

**ABE:** S.A.R.A. turns every smartphone into an environmental sensor. Three pillars: Scan. Twin. Analytics. A student points their phone at any item — our AI identifies it instantly — the scan is logged with its GPS location — and a live dashboard reveals waste hotspots across the entire campus.

### Slide 10 — Innovation 1: AI Waste Classification

**ABE:** Pillar one. Point your phone at any waste item and our Multimodal AI Vision Engine detects every object in the frame — each one gets its own bounding box, its material identified, a recyclable verdict, and a concrete instruction: rinse it, crush it, blue bin. One photo, multiple items, two seconds.

And it is resilient by design: if our primary vision model is ever unavailable, the system automatically falls back through two more models. The scanner never goes dark — built deliberately for low-connectivity environments like ours.

### Slide 11 — Innovation 2: Campus Digital Twin

**ABE:** Pillar two. Every scan feeds the first real-time waste map of any Lao university campus. GPS-tagged scans render as a live heatmap of hotspots. Smart-bin fill levels update administrators in real time — a bin flags itself as "warning" or "full" before it ever overflows.

### Slide 12 — Innovation 3: Analytics Dashboard

**NAJ:** Pillar three. Waste by type, trends over time, recycling rates, location intelligence — refreshed every fifteen seconds. A campus administrator who had zero data yesterday can now answer: what are we throwing away, where, and is our intervention actually working?

And we made the right behavior addictive. Every scan earns ReGen Points — ten per scan, capped at one hundred a day so the system rewards genuine habit, not point farming. A community feed lets students share tips and wins, because behavior change spreads socially.

### Slide 13 — Technical Architecture

**ABE:** The full pipeline, end to end: Smartphone — Multimodal AI Vision Engine — Real-Time Cloud Intelligence — Campus Digital Twin — Analytics Dashboard. It runs entirely in the browser on phones students already own. No new hardware. No app-store install. And our MVP runs on free-tier infrastructure — our burn rate is effectively zero, by strategy, not by accident.

# Q&A PREPARATION BANK

Rehearse these out loud. Whoever owns the domain answers; SIN routes questions ("Abraham will take that one").

### "Is the AI detection real? How does it work?" — ABE
"Yes — fully real. Our Multimodal AI Vision Engine analyzes the photo in a single pass: it detects every item, returns precise bounding-box coordinates for each one, identifies the material, and maps it to local disposal guidelines. For resilience we run a triple-model fallback chain — if the primary model is rate-limited or down, the system automatically retries through two more, so a student in a low-connectivity environment still gets a result."
**(Never claim two separate AI models per scan, and never name the underlying providers.)**

### "Does it work offline?" — ABE
"The scan itself needs a connection, because the heavy AI runs in the cloud — that is a deliberate choice so it works on the cheapest phones. Offline queuing is on our roadmap for the next release. What works everywhere today is the browser-based design: no app store, no install, nothing to download."

### "Is the app in Lao language?" — NAJ
"The live build is in English — the language of our pilot campus coursework. The Lao-language interface is scheduled in Phase two, this year. We deliberately validated the core loop first, then localize."

### "How is this different from existing recycling apps?" — SIN
"Three things no existing app combines: per-item AI detection at the moment of disposal, a real-time GPS digital twin that institutions act on, and multi-tenant organization management. And one thing nobody can copy: our growing dataset of real scans from a real Lao campus. Competitors have algorithms; we have real Lao data."

### "What about privacy?" — ALI
"Photos are analyzed and immediately discarded — never stored. We keep roughly two hundred bytes of text per scan: item, category, location, time. That decision came directly from our ethics coursework."

### "Your revenue is only $12,000 in year one. Why so small?" — SIN
"Because it is honest. We are at idea-to-pilot stage; year one is one to two paying campuses while the free NUOL pilot generates proof. Our five-year target of five hundred thousand dollars is twenty-four percent of a bottom-up obtainable market — ambitious but defensible, with near-zero burn rate funding our runway."

### "How will you stop students from gaming the points?" — ABE
"Already built. ReGen Points are capped at one hundred per day per user, awarded through transactional server-side logic — the system rewards daily habit, not spam. We designed for abuse from day one."

### "What is your defensible IP?" — ABE / SIN
"Two assets. First, the field-tuned classification pipeline engineered for the low-end phones common in Laos, protected as a trade secret. Second — and stronger — the compounding data loop: real usage generates unique data, unique data improves the system, and a competitor cannot shortcut years of on-the-ground presence at a Lao university."

### "How do you scale beyond one campus?" — NAT
"The platform is multi-tenant today: any university or company self-registers, gets a join code, and onboards in minutes — its own bins, its own data, its own admin panel. Scaling from one campus to fifty is sales and partnerships, not engineering."

6. **Hard constraints (from info.md Section 21):** marketing names only; only the seven verified sources; $12K year one, $500K five-year; no robotics claims; no "offline" or "Lao language" as shipped features; no "two AI models per scan."
